Finance Management in aPOP

Entities and position codes links elegantly with financial accounting. In accounting, there are four levels of organization. The Chart of Accounts (COA), the Budget Level, the Activity Level, and finally the individual Transactions. The COA is the overarching hierarchy of all transactions the whole organization can incur. For example, sales expenses are registered in the COA. In the budget level, this category is broken down into commercial expenses, sales commission, commercial expenses, conveyance expense etc. This allows the management to create a complete view of their companyâ€™s expenses while still maintaining the simplicity in their COA. In the activity level, we look at the specific instances in the real world which fall within those budget codes. The activities under the conveyance expense, for example, are the vehicles that contribute to that expense. Suppose there are three vehicles for the management, each of those vehicles is one activity. Under each activity, we track the transactions pertaining to that activity. It is at the activity level that we generate the vouchers containing all relevant transactions

The organization of financial accounts this way allows the user to generate expansive reports at the top level, while allowing the convenience to drill down to individual instances of their budgets. Once the transaction has been recorded, the debit and credit entry occurs instantaneously at the back end. Every type of manpower budget code (which is inherently linked to both entities and position codes) also have a dedicated chart of account (COA) code. This allows users to link the manpower budget codes to the COA codes and track transactions for relevant entities and positions. This mobile application also sends approval requests to the authority associated with that budget (also configured at the planning stage). This authority personnel can approve or deny this transaction before it is logged into the account statements. This submission is automatically tagged with the relevant COA and budget codes. Real time updates to the accounts allow a daily snapshot of the profitability of the business with minimal human errors and delays.
