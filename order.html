Order Management & Process Planning

Production planning begins the moment an order is received. When interacting with customers, merchandizers create a new file based on their contract requirements. This file is later broken down into relevant export orders (E.O.) which vary by delivery location and/or date. Next, these E.O.’s are grouped and planned as Production References (P. R.’s) based on strategic costminimizing decisions. This field is internal to the organization and is used only for planning purposes. The planning features extensively cover each milestone of a production order, starting from first material in-house date, all the way to last shipment date. Orders are received and instantaneously organized so that they can be planned and produced efficiently

Along the same lines as man power budgeting, production is also planned with profit in mind. Knowing the line-wise costing, and per piece contribution allows a clear idea of the production targets the entity needs to meet to stay in profit. This will be discussed in detail in upcoming documentation.

To recap, an organization structure has been created. Entities and position codes have allowed us to design manpower plans and manpower budgets. Based on the manpower budget and recruitment process sets in the position code, we have established the recruitment plans that can consist of multiple recruitment groups. Processes have been configured and a brief introduction to order management allows us to begin to generate revenue. This completes the whole business cycle from organization set-up to generating revenue. Upcoming set of documents will go in greater depth to describe how aPOP integrates even more types of business functions in a seamless digitized platform
