ERP Finance

Entities and position codes links elegantly with financial accounting. In accounting, there are four levels of organization. The Chart of Accounts (COA), the Budget Level, the Activity Level, and finally the individual Transactions. The COA is the overarching hierarchy of all transactions the whole organization can incur. For example, sales expenses are registered in the COA. In the budget level, this category is broken down into commercial expenses, sales commission, commercial expenses, conveyance expense etc. This allows the management to create a complete view of their company’s expenses while still maintaining the simplicity in their COA. In the activity level, we look at the specific instances in the real world which fall within those budget codes. The activities under the conveyance expense, for example, are the vehicles that contribute to that expense. Suppose there are three vehicles for the management, each of those vehicles is one activity. Under each activity, we track the transactions pertaining to that activity. It is at the activity level that we generate the vouchers containing all relevant transactions.

HR Recruitment

An organization is made up of systems and people. We have created a process driven application which strikes a balance between human-to-system interactions. The systems (entities, position codes, man power budgets) are in place, and now its time for its processes. Processes, too, are classified based on the entity that they are associated with. A General Process is one that does not actually directly generate revenue, and is used for the organization’s internal management. Recruitment is a non-production type process. ManPower Planning

After establishing the entities, the next step is to create position codes. Position codes are created under the divisions. The reason position codes are classified under divisions is to recognize the fact that although we have created independently functional entities, many of the job functions required in those entities can be repeated. To create unique entity-specific position codes with repeated job functions creates unnecessary complexity and takes away from transparency across the whole organization. When defined this way, we can get a holistic snapshot of the manpower in the organization despite structuring the entities using a separate criterion. This allows a greater flexibility of assigning appropriate man power to entities that may have been organized by a set of processes!

Order Management

Production planning begins the moment an order is received. When interacting with customers, merchandizers create a new file based on their contract requirements. This file is later broken down into relevant export orders (E.O.) which vary by delivery location and/or date. Next, these E.O.’s are grouped and planned as Production References (P. R.’s) based on strategic costminimizing decisions. This field is internal to the organization and is used only for planning purposes. The planning features extensively cover each milestone of a production order, starting from first material in-house date, all the way to last shipment date. Orders are received and instantaneously organized so that they can be planned and produced efficiently.
